📍 Riverside Loop Trail

🥾 3.2 miles ↑ 200 ft elevation ✅ Confidence: Easy

A shaded loop along a gentle creek — mostly flat with wide, well-marked paths. Ideal for your first outing. Parking is easy and the trailhead has restrooms.

💡 Quick Tip: Start early on summer weekends — the parking lot fills by 9 AM. Bring water even though it feels short.

📍 Pine Ridge Path

🥾 4.8 miles ↑ 480 ft elevation ✅ Confidence: Moderate

A slightly longer out-and-back through pine forest with a rewarding ridgeline viewpoint at the halfway mark. The incline is steady but never steep — great for building stamina.

💡 Quick Tip: Download an offline map before you go — cell service is spotty past the first mile. Trekking poles make the descent easier on your knees.

Weather Checklist

  • Check forecast the night before
  • Re-check morning of the hike
  • Note afternoon storm risk
  • Pack a rain layer just in case
  • Plan to be off ridgelines by noon

Safety Reminders

  • Tell someone your trailhead and return time
  • Bring more water than you think you need
  • Download an offline map before you leave
  • Charge your phone fully the night before
  • Know when to turn back — no summit is worth a rescue
